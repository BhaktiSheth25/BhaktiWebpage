Concept
The entire concept of this project is that we have to chosen a brand, social cause and Indian art forms, through which we have to make a poster highlighting the social cause that we had decided. Forest Essentials is an authentic, traditional skincare brand with its foundations in the ancient science of Ayurveda. A pioneer of Luxurious Ayurveda, today it has become the perfect example of Indian beauty brand that combines the ancient beauty rituals of Ayurveda with a stylish, modern aesthetic for a more relevant emphasis on efficacy, sensorial experience and pleasure of usage.

History of Design

Posters for Cup Prints

Concept -
This project comprises of eight poster, each representing a particular movement that have led to the evolution of design.
So the first poster represent 'The Baroque Movement' which was considered to be more of richness,use of gold color, overdecorated and stylistic complex designs.
The second movement was 'The Rococo Movement' which used brighter tone to give a appealing look to the eye along with making it look playful and ostentatious.
The third movement was 'The Impressionism Movement' which was all about visible small and thin brush strokes along with emphasing on lighting which makes the painting look brighter and more realistic.
The fourth movement was 'The Art Nouveau Movement' which aimed at modernizing design, seeking to escape the eclectic historical styles that had previously been popular.
The fifth movement was 'The Cubism Movement' which was all about exploring open form, piercing figures and objects by letting the space flow through them, blending background into foreground, and showing objects from various angles.
The sixth movement was 'The Art Deco Movement' which shows that strength is arguably that it combines, borrows from, and takes inspiration from so many other design styles across the spectrum.
The seventh movement was 'The Abstraction Movement' which was all about encouraging both artists and the public to think in new ways and to reflect on both the expansive and the minimal and to seek new perspectives.
And the last movement was 'The Minimalist Movement' which is about the culmination of reductionist tendencies in modern art that first surfaced in the 1913.
